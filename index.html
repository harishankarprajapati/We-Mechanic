Frequently Asked Questions

A car battery typically lasts 3 to 5 years, depending on factors like driving habits, climate conditions, vehicle usage, and regular maintenance. Extreme temperatures and frequent short trips can shorten its lifespan.

Common signs include slow engine cranking, dim headlights, battery warning light on the dashboard, electrical issues, and difficulty starting the car, especially in cold weather.

You can extend your car battery’s life by driving regularly, avoiding short trips, keeping battery terminals clean, turning off electronics when the engine is off, and getting the battery checked during routine servicing.
